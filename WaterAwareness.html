🔍 Key insight: Only ~2.5% of Earth's water is fresh, and of that fresh water, ~68.7% is locked in glaciers and ~30% is groundwater. Less than 0.3% of all fresh water is readily accessible in rivers, lakes, and shallow aquifers for human use. This makes efficient water management and desalination essential for global drinking water supply.
